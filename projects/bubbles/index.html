What problem & for whom

For whom: People who want a quick, satisfying break—no story, no sign-up. Just tap and pop.

Problem: Mobile games are either too heavy (downloads, ads, accounts) or too shallow. They want something that feels good in the moment and doesn't ask for anything.

This: One screen. Pop bubbles before they float away. Special ones burst into glitter. One tap to play, a timer for tension, score at the end. Play again or leave.

Design decisions

What AI could and couldn't do: I wanted bubbles to feel physical—slight drift, varied sizes, a bit of randomness. The core loop (spawn, tap, pop, score) is pure JS and canvas; AI helped with structure and copy. The glitter burst on special bubbles was a design choice to give a clear "win" moment without adding complexity.

Designing for the obvious: One button to start. A visible timer so you know the stakes. Score only at the end so you're not distracted. No instructions—the first tap is on "Play."

Minimum viable effort: No levels, no unlocks. One round, one score. If you want more, you hit "Play Again." That's it.
