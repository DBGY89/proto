1. Actual: cierre de Design decisions (antes de Get in touch)

From idea to prototype

Design decisions

The game that couldn't be: I wanted ingredients to appear visually on a plate… The plate didn't make it. That taught me something: AI is better at language than at spatial visualization, at least today. The game moved, not died.

From list to interface: The original layout was a vertical stack of boxes. I moved to a side-by-side layout on desktop so both panels are visible at once.

The instruction nobody reads: The interface does tell you what to do. There's a line asking you to pick at least one ingredient. But let's be honest, most users won't read it. I added an animation that highlights the ingredients one by one, making the first move obvious just in case.

Minimum viable effort: One click = one full salad with dressing and recipe. I kept asking myself "can I remove one more step?" until the answer was no.

Built in two evenings.
