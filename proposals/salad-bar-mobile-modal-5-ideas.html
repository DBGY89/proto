Salad Bar

Build your salad. We'll match it with a dressing…

From idea to prototype

What problem & for whom

For whom: People who open the fridge, see perfectly good vegetables, and still end up with olive oil and salt — because creativity in the kitchen doesn't come naturally to everyone.

Problem: "I have lettuce, tomato, avocado — vinaigrette? Yogurt?" They want a quick answer and a real recipe. No guesswork, no sign-up, no friction.

This: Pick ingredients → get a matching dressing + recipe + one kitchen trick. One click. Done.

Design decisions

The game that couldn't be: I wanted ingredients to appear visually on a plate as you picked them. The AI only rendered colored dots (red for tomato, green for cucumber). The plate didn't make it. The game moved, not died — the playful layer shifted to the ingredient animation instead.

From list to interface: I moved to a side-by-side layout on desktop. On mobile, the output box peeks from below — a deliberate cue to reduce friction.

Zero instructions: Animation that highlights ingredients one by one. First interaction in under 3 seconds, zero reading required.

Minimum viable effort: One click = one full salad with dressing and recipe.

1. Bottom sheet — Actual: panel desde abajo, handle, scroll. Familiar en iOS/Android.

2. Full screen — Modal ocupa toda la pantalla. Más foco, sin sensación de “capita”.

3. Tabs — Mismo sheet pero con pestañas Problem | Story | Contact. Menos scroll, salto directo.

4. Drawer right — Panel entra desde la derecha (~92% ancho). Sensación de “detalle” o “panel lateral”.

5. Accordion — Secciones colapsables. El sheet puede empezar más bajo; se expande al tocar cada bloque.
